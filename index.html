HONORARY MAMMAL

Kiwi birds have really fluffy, hair-like feathers that help them camouflage in the woods. They also make them SO SOFT.

Often classified as an "honorary mammal" due to its divergent characteristics from typical avian species, the kiwi possesses a number of unusual features. Its dense, heavy bones, filled with marrow, and powerful legs, constituting a third of its body weight, enable it to achieve remarkable running speeds rivaling those of humans.
